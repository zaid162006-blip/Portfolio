Gnosis - Olympus Edition

As Event Head, I directed and filmed the cinematic teaser trailer for GNOSIS – Olympus Edition, my college’s annual STEM event. The video followed a mysterious, thriller-like narrative, building suspense around the unveiling of the event. By combining symbolic storytelling with strong visuals, the trailer established GNOSIS as more than just a competition — it became an experience that students were drawn into.

[Client]

BCP Johar Town

[Year]

2024

[Services]

Creative Direction, Production, Post Production

[Catagory]

Social Media

Project Objectives:


  • Produce a cinematic teaser trailer to build hype for GNOSIS – Olympus Edition, my college’s annual STEM event.

  • Create a story-driven video that reflects the intellectual and mysterious nature of the competition.

  • Strengthen the event’s digital presence and attract a wider audience of students.

Project Scope:

Content Planning:


  • Developed a mysterious, thriller-like storyline to connect the trailer with the investigative and problem-solving essence of STEM.

  • Outlined the mood, pacing, and shots to ensure a cinematic reveal.

Production:


  • Directed and shot the trailer using controlled lighting and symbolic props to establish suspense.

  • Utilized creative camera angles and movement to enhance tension and storytelling.

Post-production:


  • Edited the footage with cinematic color grading and dramatic sound design.

  • Built momentum through pacing, transitions, and music to deliver a suspenseful final cut.

Results:

he teaser successfully established GNOSIS – Olympus Edition as more than just a competition — it became an experience shrouded in mystery and excitement. Alongside the trailer, I also created promotional Instagram reels that went viral, with one reel reaching 5.2M views and several others crossing six-figure engagements. This virality gave GNOSIS unprecedented reach and visibility.

Lessons Learned:


  • Storytelling is essential to elevate promotional trailers beyond simple announcements.

  • Visual tone and atmosphere can strongly influence how an academic event is perceived.

  • Social-first content like reels can extend the reach of events far beyond their campus audience.
